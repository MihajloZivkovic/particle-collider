ABOUT PROJECT

12.10.2024: start of project;

The basic idea is to build a simulation of what happens with a few basic nucleus collisons. The user should select the target and the projectile with given interface, and there should be an animation with those particles coliding. After the collision, there should be a picture with all the products of it as well as a summary explaining what happened. Optional: On every important word or name of particle(when reading the summary), refer to a wikipedia page or a quick explanation of the properties, mass, spin, etc...

22.10.2024: update;

So most of the visual work is finished, the particle selection is working as intended and its detected when not both(or none) of the particles are selected. One major roadblock i ran into was picking which particles to collide, firstly i wanted to simulate a high-energy collision, but it was very difficult for me to find/understand the data of them, so i will resort to low energy well known collisions. The goal with this project was for it to be a *very* primitive interactive type sandbox simulation, so i will have 4 types of particles(proton electron and their antiparticles) per side.

29.10.2024: update;

The particle collider demo is finished, i am pretty happy how it turned out, but most importantly im proud of myself for having enough will power to finish the project.
